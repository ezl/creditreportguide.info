Welcome!

Credit reports can be complicated and difficult to understand.

This is an interactive guide to help you understand how to effectively read credit reports.

If you hover your mouse over any content that is highlighted in beige on the sample credit report to your left, you'll see an explanation of what that data means right here.


Name

This is the applicant's name. It's usually a bunch of letters and spaces.

Date Issued

The date the credit report was ordered.

Credit Score

The applicant's FICO score is a measure of his or her creditworthiness.

It's traditionally used to determine someone's likelhood to repay a debt like a mortgage or a car loan, but is commonly used in the rental industry for helping determine if an applicant is likely to pay rent on time.

Scores range from 350-850. Anything over a 700 is considered excellent credit worthiness in most states.

Trade Lines

Notes about tradelines!

Credit Guarantor

The credit guarantor is the actual creditor for a given account. This is a person, company, or organization where the applicant has a debt or an open account.

It could be a credit card company, bank, mortgage, etc.

Current Status

This is the status of the account, i.e. whether it's in good standing and current on all payments, behind on payments, has been written off, etc.

If you see "Current", that's good. If you see "Chargeoff", that's bad.
